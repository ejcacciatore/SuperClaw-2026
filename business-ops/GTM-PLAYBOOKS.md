# GTM Playbooks — Go-to-Market Strategies

**How to get customers for different business types.**

---

## Enterprise SaaS (CalcGuard)

### Profile
- **Deal size:** $10K - $100K+ ARR
- **Sales cycle:** 3-12 months
- **Buyer:** VP/Director level
- **Decision process:** Committee, procurement, legal

### Playbook

#### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)
1. **Build credibility assets**
   - Case studies (pilot customers)
   - Thought leadership (LinkedIn posts, articles)
   - Technical content (white papers, architecture docs)

2. **Define ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**
   - Industry: Asset managers, brokers, trading desks
   - Size: $500M+ AUM
   - Pain: Data fragmentation, execution quality blind spots
   - Budget authority: CTO, Head of Trading Tech

3. **Build outbound list**
   - 100 target accounts
   - Find champions (traders, analysts, risk managers)
   - Multi-threaded approach (3+ contacts per account)

#### Phase 2: Outbound (Months 4-6)
1. **Cold outreach**
   - LinkedIn → warm intro request
   - Email → problem-focused (not product pitch)
   - Follow-up sequence (5-7 touches)

2. **Content marketing**
   - LinkedIn posts (2-3x/week on market structure, data challenges)
   - Guest articles (TabbFORUM, Markets Media)
   - Conference speaking (industry events)

3. **Partnerships**
   - System integrators (consultants who sell to your ICP)
   - Complementary vendors (OMS, EMS, TCA providers)
   - Industry associations (SIFMA, FIA)

#### Phase 3: Close (Months 7-12)
1. **Demo → Pilot → Contract**
   - Discovery call (understand pain, budget, timeline)
   - Custom demo (show their data, not generic)
   - Pilot (30-60 days, paid if possible)
   - Pricing discussion (anchor high, negotiate down)
   - Legal/procurement (be patient, this takes weeks)

2. **Metrics to track**
   - Pipeline coverage (3x quota minimum)
   - Conversion rates (demo → pilot → close)
   - Sales cycle length (optimize over time)

## Consulting (Sapinover)

### Profile
- **Deal size:** $5K - $50K per project
- **Sales cycle:** 2-8 weeks
- **Buyer:** CISO, COO, VP Operations
- **Decision process:** Single decision-maker or small committee

### Playbook

#### Phase 1: Positioning (Months 1-2)
1. **Niche down**
   - Target: Enterprises, govt contractors, critical infrastructure
   - Capability: Operational resilience, IC/SOCOM expertise
   - Differentiator: Real-world experience, not just theory

2. **Build portfolio**
   - Case studies (anonymized if needed)
   - Testimonials (from past clients/colleagues)
   - Certifications (if relevant: CISSP, etc.)

3. **Website clarity**
   - Services page (exactly what you do)
   - Pricing guidance (at least ballpark)
   - CTA (book consultation, not generic "contact")

#### Phase 2: Inbound (Ongoing)
1. **Content marketing**
   - LinkedIn posts (2x/week on security, resilience)
   - Long-form articles (Medium, company blog)
   - Guest posts (industry publications)

2. **Speaking/teaching**
   - Webinars (host or guest)
   - Conferences (submit CFPs)
   - Workshops (training sessions)

3. **SEO**
   - Target keywords (e.g., "operational resilience consulting")
   - Local SEO (if targeting specific geography)
   - Backlinks (guest posts, partnerships)

#### Phase 3: Outbound (Ongoing)
1. **Warm outreach**
   - Referrals (from past clients, colleagues)
   - LinkedIn connections (2nd degree, warm intros)
   - Industry Slack/Discord channels

2. **Cold outreach**
   - Targeted emails (research-first, personalized)
   - LinkedIn messages (comment on posts first)
   - Conference follow-ups

#### Phase 4: Close (Weeks 1-4)
1. **Discovery call**
   - Understand problem, budget, urgency
   - Position as expert (teach, don't sell)
   - Define scope clearly

2. **Proposal**
   - Problem statement
   - Approach/methodology
   - Timeline and deliverables
   - Pricing (fixed or hourly with cap)
   - Terms (payment schedule, cancellation)

3. **Negotiate → Close**
   - Handle objections (budget, timeline, alternatives)
   - Offer pilot (smaller engagement first)
   - Close verbally, follow with contract

## Ecommerce/Marketplace (World Specialties)

### Profile
- **AOV:** $50 - $200
- **CAC:** $20 - $50 (paid ads)
- **Conversion rate:** 1-3% (cold traffic)
- **Repeat rate:** 20-40%

### Playbook

#### Phase 1: Product-Market Fit (Months 1-3)
1. **Curate initial inventory**
   - 20-50 SKUs (tight niche)
   - Unique, hard-to-find, story-driven
   - Test pricing (aim for 40%+ margin)

2. **Build MVP site**
   - Shopify or similar (fast setup)
   - High-quality photos
   - Compelling product descriptions (storytelling)
   - Mobile-optimized

3. **Test with organic traffic**
   - SEO (product pages optimized)
   - Social media (Instagram, Pinterest)
   - Email to warm audience (friends, family)

#### Phase 2: Paid Acquisition (Months 4-6)
1. **Meta ads (Instagram/Facebook)**
   - Start with $500/mo budget
   - Test audiences (interests, lookalikes)
   - Optimize for conversions (not clicks)

2. **Google Shopping**
   - Product feed (automated)
   - Target high-intent keywords
   - ROAS target: 3x minimum

3. **Influencer partnerships**
   - Micro-influencers (1K-50K followers)
   - Commission-based (10-20% of sales)
   - Authentic fit (not generic promotion)

#### Phase 3: Retention (Months 7-12)
1. **Email marketing**
   - Welcome series (3-5 emails)
   - Abandoned cart (recover 10-20% of lost sales)
   - Post-purchase (upsell, cross-sell)
   - Reactivation (win back churned customers)

2. **Loyalty program**
   - Points/rewards (encourage repeat)
   - VIP tier (top 10% of customers)
   - Referral bonus (incentivize word-of-mouth)

3. **Content**
   - Blog (SEO + storytelling)
   - Social media (behind-the-scenes, vendor stories)
   - Video (product demos, maker interviews)

## Platform/Marketplace (KeyLedger)

### Profile
- **Chicken-and-egg:** Need buyers AND sellers/agents
- **Liquidity:** Critical mass required for value
- **Network effects:** Gets better with more users

### Playbook

#### Phase 1: Solve Supply First (Months 1-3)
1. **Manual curation**
   - Recruit 10-20 agents (hand-picked, high-quality)
   - Offer incentives (free tool, commission boost, exclusive access)
   - Build agent tools (dashboard, analytics, CRM)

2. **Value prop clarity**
   - For agents: More leads, faster close, better tools
   - For buyers: Verified sellers, streamlined process, trust
   - For sellers: Qualified buyers, faster transactions

3. **Start hyper-local**
   - One city or market (e.g., Fairfield County, CT)
   - Deep penetration (become go-to in that area)
   - Expand only after liquidity achieved

#### Phase 2: Attract Demand (Months 4-6)
1. **SEO**
   - Target keywords (e.g., "homes for sale in Fairfield CT")
   - Listings pages (indexed by Google)
   - Content (neighborhood guides, market reports)

2. **Paid ads**
   - Google (high-intent: "buy house in [city]")
   - Meta (awareness: "thinking of buying?")
   - Retargeting (convert browsers to leads)

3. **Partnerships**
   - Mortgage brokers (referral fees)
   - Movers, inspectors (cross-promote)
   - Local businesses (co-marketing)

#### Phase 3: Build Liquidity (Months 7-12)
1. **Metrics to watch**
   - Supply/demand balance (1:10 sellers:buyers ideal)
   - Match rate (% of listings that get offers)
   - Time to close (faster = better experience)

2. **Incentivize behavior**
   - Agents: Bonuses for fast response, high rating
   - Buyers: Rewards for completing profile, booking tours
   - Sellers: Pricing incentives for verified listings

3. **Expand carefully**
   - Add adjacent markets (one at a time)
   - Only when existing market has liquidity
   - Replicate playbook (don't start from scratch)

## API/Developer Product (CalcGuard API)

### Profile
- **Buyer:** Engineering team, CTO
- **Decision:** Technical first, then commercial
- **Adoption:** Bottom-up (devs try it, then buy)

### Playbook

#### Phase 1: Developer Experience (Months 1-3)
1. **Docs**
   - Quick start (5 min to first API call)
   - API reference (every endpoint, parameter, response)
   - SDKs (Python, JavaScript, others as needed)
   - Code examples (real-world use cases)

2. **Sandbox**
   - Free tier (generous limits for testing)
   - Test data (sample datasets)
   - No credit card required (remove friction)

3. **Support**
   - Slack/Discord community
   - Fast response time (<4h for critical)
   - Public roadmap (transparency builds trust)

#### Phase 2: Awareness (Months 4-6)
1. **Content**
   - Technical blog posts (how to use API, best practices)
   - Tutorials (step-by-step integrations)
   - Case studies (customer success stories)

2. **Distribution**
   - GitHub (open-source SDKs, example repos)
   - Dev forums (Stack Overflow, Reddit)
   - Podcasts/videos (technical deep dives)

3. **SEO**
   - Target keywords (e.g., "trading data API", "execution quality API")
   - Comparison pages (vs competitors)
   - Landing pages (per use case)

#### Phase 3: Conversion (Months 7-12)
1. **Freemium → Paid**
   - Usage alerts (notify when nearing limit)
   - Upgrade prompts (in dashboard, email)
   - Sales outreach (for high-usage free users)

2. **Enterprise sales**
   - Reach out to heavy users (offer custom contract)
   - SLAs, priority support, custom integrations
   - Annual contracts (prepay discount)

3. **Upsell**
   - Add-ons (premium data, advanced analytics)
   - Higher tiers (more calls, faster rate limits)
   - White-label (resell to their customers)

## Content/Media (Free Range Democracy)

### Profile
- **Monetization:** Long-term (build audience first)
- **Revenue:** Ads, subscriptions, products, affiliate
- **Timeline:** 12-24 months to meaningful income

### Playbook

#### Phase 1: Audience Building (Months 1-6)
1. **Consistency**
   - Post schedule (weekly minimum)
   - Quality > quantity (deep, insightful)
   - Niche down (political commentary, not generic news)

2. **Distribution**
   - Twitter/X (engagement, threads)
   - LinkedIn (long-form, professional)
   - Substack (email ownership)
   - Medium (SEO, cross-post)

3. **Engagement**
   - Respond to comments
   - Join conversations (reply to others' posts)
   - Collaborate (guest posts, podcasts)

#### Phase 2: Email List (Months 7-12)
1. **Lead magnet**
   - Free report, guide, checklist
   - Offer on every post
   - Landing page (capture emails)

2. **Newsletter**
   - Weekly or bi-weekly
   - Mix of content (articles, curated links, commentary)
   - Personal voice (build connection)

3. **Segmentation**
   - Tag by interest (policy, economics, social)
   - Personalize content
   - Track engagement (open rate, clicks)

#### Phase 3: Monetization (Months 13-24)
1. **Paid subscriptions**
   - Premium content (deeper analysis, early access)
   - Patreon, Substack, Ghost
   - Price: $5-$10/mo

2. **Affiliate**
   - Book recommendations (Amazon)
   - Tools/services (referral fees)
   - Disclose clearly (trust = critical)

3. **Products**
   - Ebook (compilation of best posts)
   - Course (teach a skill)
   - Consulting (productize expertise)
