# Business Models — Revenue Frameworks

**How different business models generate revenue, scale, and achieve product-market fit.**

---

## SaaS (Software as a Service)

### Examples
- CalcGuard Technologies (data fabric for institutional trading)
- KeyLedger (real estate transaction intelligence)

### Revenue Model
- **Monthly/annual subscriptions** ($100 - $100K+ per customer)
- Tiered pricing (Starter, Pro, Enterprise)
- Usage-based add-ons (API calls, data volume, users)

### Key Metrics
- **MRR/ARR** — Monthly/Annual Recurring Revenue
- **CAC** — Customer Acquisition Cost
- **LTV** — Lifetime Value (target: LTV/CAC > 3)
- **Churn** — Monthly customer attrition (target: <5%)
- **NRR** — Net Revenue Retention (target: >110%)

### GTM Motion
- **Self-serve** (PLG): Free trial → paid conversion
- **Sales-led** (Enterprise): Demo → pilot → contract
- **Hybrid**: Free tier + sales for Enterprise

### Unit Economics
- Gross margin: 70-90%
- Payback period: <12 months
- Break-even: typically 18-36 months

### Moats
- Network effects (more users = more value)
- Data moats (proprietary data sets)
- Integration lock-in (hard to switch)
- Brand/trust (in regulated industries)

## Consulting/Services

### Examples
- Sapinover (operational resilience consulting)

### Revenue Model
- **Hourly/daily rates** ($200/hr - $2K/day)
- **Fixed-price projects** ($5K - $500K)
- **Retainers** ($5K/mo - $50K/mo)

### Key Metrics
- **Utilization rate** — billable hours / total hours (target: 60-80%)
- **Effective rate** — revenue / hours worked
- **Client lifetime value** — total revenue per client
- **Repeat rate** — % of clients who return

### GTM Motion
- Inbound (content, referrals, speaking)
- Outbound (cold email, LinkedIn, conferences)
- Partnerships (channel, referral)

### Unit Economics
- Gross margin: 40-70% (after contractor costs)
- No recurring revenue (unless retainer)
- Scales linearly with headcount

### Moats
- Reputation/portfolio
- Proprietary methodologies
- Domain expertise (IC/SOCOM background)
- Client relationships

## Ecommerce/Marketplace

### Examples
- World Specialties (artisan goods marketplace)

### Revenue Model
- **Product sales** (retail margin: 20-50%)
- **Marketplace commission** (10-30% of GMV)
- **Listing fees** (flat or percentage)
- **Premium placement** (advertising)

### Key Metrics
- **GMV** — Gross Merchandise Value (total sales)
- **Take rate** — % of GMV you capture
- **CAC** — Customer Acquisition Cost
- **AOV** — Average Order Value
- **Repeat purchase rate** — % of customers who buy again

### GTM Motion
- SEO (organic search traffic)
- Paid ads (Google, Meta, TikTok)
- Influencer partnerships
- Email marketing

### Unit Economics
- Gross margin: 30-50% (after COGS, shipping)
- High CAC if paid acquisition
- Scales with inventory + marketing spend

### Moats
- Brand (curated selection, trust)
- Supply relationships (exclusive vendors)
- Logistics efficiency (fast/cheap shipping)
- Network effects (more vendors = more buyers)

---

## Platform/Marketplace (Two-Sided)

### Examples
- KeyLedger (buyers + sellers + agents)
- CalcGuard (data providers + data consumers)

### Revenue Model
- **Commission** (% of transaction value)
- **Subscription** (from one or both sides)
- **Listing fees** (from suppliers)
- **Data/API access** (from consumers)

### Key Metrics
- **GMV** — Gross Merchandise Value
- **Active users** (both sides)
- **Liquidity** — supply/demand balance
- **NPS** — Net Promoter Score (critical for multi-sided)

### GTM Motion
- **Chicken-and-egg problem** — need supply AND demand
- Solve by:
  1. Subsidize one side (free for sellers, paid for buyers)
  2. Start hyper-local (one city, one niche)
  3. Manual curation (hand-pick both sides)

### Unit Economics
- High initial CAC (need to bootstrap both sides)
- Low marginal cost once liquidity achieved
- Very high margins at scale (80%+)

### Moats
- Network effects (strongest moat if achieved)
- Liquidity (hard to compete once established)
- Data/insights (transaction history = intelligence)

## API/Data Products

### Examples
- CalcGuard (data normalization + analytics APIs)

### Revenue Model
- **Pay-per-call** ($0.01 - $1 per API call)
- **Tiered plans** (10K calls/mo, 1M calls/mo)
- **Enterprise contracts** (custom pricing, SLAs)

### Key Metrics
- **API calls/month** — usage volume
- **Active integrations** — # of customers using API
- **Uptime** — 99.9%+ critical
- **P50/P95 latency** — response time

### GTM Motion
- Developer-first (docs, SDKs, sandbox)
- Freemium (free tier for testing)
- Self-serve signup → sales for Enterprise

### Unit Economics
- Very high gross margin (85-95%)
- Low CAC (PLG model)
- Scales with usage (not headcount)

### Moats
- Data quality/uniqueness
- API reliability (switching cost = downtime)
- Integration depth (hard to rip out)
- Developer ecosystem (community, tools)

## Content/Media

### Examples
- Free Range Democracy (commentary blog)

### Revenue Model
- **Advertising** (display, sponsored)
- **Subscriptions** (Substack, Patreon)
- **Affiliate commissions** (product recs)
- **Courses/products** (productized expertise)

### Key Metrics
- **Traffic** — monthly pageviews
- **Engagement** — time on site, comments
- **Email list** — subscribers (most valuable)
- **RPM** — Revenue per 1000 views

### GTM Motion
- SEO (organic search)
- Social media (Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Guest posts/podcasts
- Viral content

### Unit Economics
- Very low margin if ad-based (20-40%)
- High margin if subscription (60-80%)
- Scales with content volume + distribution

### Moats
- Audience/email list (owned channel)
- Brand/voice (hard to replicate)
- SEO authority (backlinks, domain age)

## Decision Matrix: Which Model for Which Business?

| Business | Model | Reasoning |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| **CalcGuard** | SaaS + API | Recurring revenue, scales with customers, enterprise pricing |
| **Sapinover** | Consulting + Retainer | Expertise-based, high-touch, project/retainer hybrid |
| **World Specialties** | Ecommerce | Physical goods, marketplace model for vendor scale |
| **KeyLedger** | Platform/SaaS | Two-sided (buyers/sellers/agents), network effects |
| **Free Range Democracy** | Content | Audience-building, monetize later (ads/products) |

## Mixing Models

**Many successful businesses combine models:**

### SaaS + Consulting
- Example: CalcGuard SaaS + implementation services
- Pros: Higher revenue per customer, easier upsell
- Cons: Doesn't scale as cleanly

### Marketplace + SaaS
- Example: KeyLedger marketplace + agent SaaS tools
- Pros: Multiple revenue streams, stickier
- Cons: Complex to build/operate

### Content + Products
- Example: Free Range Democracy blog + courses
- Pros: Audience trust converts to sales
- Cons: Slow build, needs consistent output

## Revenue Benchmarks by Stage

### Pre-Revenue (Validation)
- Goal: 10 customers willing to pay
- Metric: LOIs, pre-orders, pilot agreements
- Timeline: 0-6 months

### Early Revenue (PMF Search)
- Goal: $10K MRR or $100K ARR
- Metric: Consistent growth, low churn
- Timeline: 6-18 months

### Growth (Scaling)
- Goal: $100K MRR or $1M ARR
- Metric: Repeatable sales process, unit economics proven
- Timeline: 18-36 months

### Scale (Dominant)
- Goal: $1M+ MRR or $10M+ ARR
- Metric: Market leadership, strong moats
- Timeline: 36+ months

## When to Pivot Models

**Signals you might need to change:**
- ❌ Can't hit $10K MRR after 12 months
- ❌ CAC > LTV (losing money on every customer)
- ❌ Can't retain customers (high churn)
- ❌ Market too small (TAM < $100M)

**Consider:**
- Consulting → SaaS (productize your service)
- SaaS → Platform (add marketplace/network)
- API → Full solution (move up the value chain)
- Content → Course/SaaS (monetize expertise)
